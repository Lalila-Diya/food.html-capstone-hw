Tasty Food

Food can be described by its taste, such as sweet, sour, salty, or savory; its texture, like creamy, crunchy, chewy, or flaky; or its overall quality, such as delicious, bland, or rich. Describing food involves using adjectives to communicate the sensory experience, from how it smells and tastes to how it feels in your mouth.

Speghetti

Spaghetti is a long, solid, cylindrical pasta made from durum wheat and water, forming a staple of Italian cuisine. Its name comes from the Italian word spaghetto, meaning "little string". It is typically boiled and served with various sauces, such as tomato, meat, or vegetables, and can be enjoyed on its own or as part of a larger dish.

Ingredients
12 ounces (about 340g) spaghetti 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil 5 large garlic cloves, minced (divided) 1 (15 ounce/425g) can tomato sauce, low sodium 2 1/2 teaspoons salt (divided) Ground black pepper, to taste 2 teaspoons dried oregano Parmesan cheese, freshly grated, for garnish Fresh basil or parsley, for garnish (optional)

Pasta

Pasta is a versatile and popular food, typically made from a dough of wheat flour, water, and sometimes eggs. It is a staple of Italian cuisine but is now enjoyed in various forms around the world. There are hundreds of shapes, which are generally categorized into two main types: dried and fresh.

Ingredients
1½ cups Penne Pasta (or similar shape) 6 cups Water (for boiling pasta) 1 tsp Salt (for boiling pasta) 2 tbsp Olive oil (divided) ½ tsp Cumin seeds (jeera) Minced garlic and ginger Finely chopped onions Chopped or pureed tomatoes Chopped carrots, green peas, and capsicum (bell pepper) Salt (to taste), Kashmiri red chili powder, turmeric (optional), garam masala Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) or fresh coriander leaves Optional: grated Cheese or heavy cream for a creamy version

Pasta

A steak is a thick slice of meat, typically beef, cut against the muscle fibers and cooked by grilling, frying, or broiling. It can also refer to other meats like fish or even non-meat items like mushrooms, but beef steak is the most common. Cooking steak properly involves choosing the right cut based on flavor and tenderness, seasoning it well, and cooking it to the desired internal temperature, such as rare, medium-rare, or well-done.

Ingredients
1 to 2 thick-cut steaks (1–1½ inches thick), such as ribeye or New York strip 1 tbsp high-smoke-point oil, like vegetable, canola, or extra-light olive oil 2–4 tbsp unsalted butter 2 cloves garlic, peeled and lightly smashed 2 sprigs fresh rosemary or thyme Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

A burger, or hamburger, is a sandwich consisting of a cooked patty, usually meat, placed between two halves of a bun. It is a popular fast food and can be customized with a variety of toppings like cheese, lettuce, tomato, and onions, along with condiments such as ketchup and mustard. While the term "burger" often implies a beef patty, other variations like chicken or vegetarian burgers are common.

Ingredients
½ cup panko breadcrumbs 1 large egg 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce 1 tbsp ketchup Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste 2 tbsp olive oil (for pan-frying)
